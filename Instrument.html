This image shows the extra rods located on the tenor joint of the instrument. These are the main rods that move as the system operates.

This image shows a small "finger" that links the bass and tenor joint of the instrument together, and is activated when the Eb key is pressed down.

This image shows an extra ring around the first finger tone hole on the boot joint. This ring is the main activator of the system.

Lloyd Waller plays on a Fox 201 built in the early 2000's. Lloyd Waller's Instrument is rare by virture of the fact it has the Wiesberg System added to it, a system which eleminates the need for "flicking" and adds greater smoothness and flexibility to the upper register.

This image shows the main part of the system; a set of two pads that operate on a pivot point. When the previously shown rinig is pressed down, the pad that is currently down is lifted, letting a small amount of air out and eliminating the need to "flick".
