Reeds

Lloyd Waller begins by profiling his cane on a Rieger Profiler, which he then shapes using a Fox Number 2 Shaper.

Lloyd Waller uses a "coccoon" method to form his reeds, and they will often stay in this form for up to a year until they are ready to be further formed.

Lloyd Waller uses four wires on his reeds to prevent leaking and improve response.

Lloyd Waller plays on rather traditional reeds, beginning with cane he acquires from Jim Ewen, owner of Skookum Reeds, located at 348 Oakwood Ave., Winnipeg, MB, Canada R3L 1G1. His business email is jimewen@mymts.net.

Finally, to complete his reeds Lloyd Waller uses hot glue to seal the reeds, as this has proven to be an effective method to prevent leaking and hold the reed together, as well as add a surface to hold on to when placing the reed onto the bocal.
